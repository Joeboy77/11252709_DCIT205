Administrative Resources for Faculty,
Staff and Students

Faculty Resources

Faculty Handbook: You can access the faculty's Handbook by clicking on this link Faculty Handbook

Hiring People:  

Please be aware that in order to start working at University of Ghana, every employee who is paid must have a social security number and submit an I-9 form. Undergraduate and graduate students from abroad must apply for a Social Security number (SSN) through the Social Security office. Do not ask anyone to start working until this phase is finished since it may take some time.

Research Staff Appointments: Appointments for research workers (such as visiting researchers, postdocs, and research staff) The UG Customer Service Center manages visitor appointments and research. Please make sure you have office or desk space for your visitor before requesting an appointment for research workers or visitors. Please see the University of Ghana's website for details on how to start the procedure after you have chosen a candidate. Every request is made by pad@ug.edu.gh.

Hosting an Event:
If you will be hosting short-term visitors and would require a parking pass or an office for meetings during their visit, please get in touch with the HOD. To allow time for arrangements, please give at least one week's notice. Fill out the Event Request form if you would want talks or other event support. To locate a day that works for your event, visit the CS Events page. Note that some events might not be posted publicly and are still in the planning stages.

Student Resources

Department Library:
Aside the University's laid libraries, the department has a library with furnished books to enhance academic work. The library is opened for students within week days.

COMPSA:
The Computer Science Students Association (COMPSA) is an association for all students in the department. The association organizes annual elections and congregations which is approved by the HOD. The COMPSA is an independent body which is administered by students with selected staff from the department

Software Lab
The softwre lab is opened to both faculty staff and students. It enhances softwre development both frontend and backend software development. Groups or individuals from the faculty who want to use the lab should contact the HOD.
Hackaton:
A Student who wishes to be part of the annual department's Hackaton event should contact the COMPSA executives for registration
Regulations:
Students can access rules and regulations in the University through this link
Student-Lecturer Relationship:
Establishing a student-lecturer relationship is crucial for academic success and personal growth. The department has established a committee to resolve issues concerning students and their respective lecturers. Department members can contact Mark Attah Mensah the head of the committee for any issues

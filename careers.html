CAREERS

Careers in Computer Science

The University of Ghana is persisting in its grand endeavor to expand and revolutionize the field of Computer Science (CS). Twenty new tenure-track faculty members in core computer science and in (CS+X) are being added to the department, along with many non-tenure-track teaching faculty members. We are looking for exceptional people who are thrilled about the chance to contribute to the development of computer science at a top university.

Open positions

Assistant Professor of Computer Science

We are currently accepting applications for our open assistant professor of computer science posts. Applications from exceptional individuals in all fields of computer science are welcome. The areas of computational photography, computer vision, machine learning, parallel systems, and theoretical computer science are areas in which the department is particularly interested in expanding.

We also welcome individuals and teams interested in pushing the boundaries of computation research to apply for faculty positions in CS + X in collaboration with other UG schools or departments. We have hired in tandem with the Segal Design Institute, Mechanical Engineering, Economics, Preventive Medicine, Statistics, Learning Sciences, Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation, and Communication Studies over the past few years. We are still dedicated to interdisciplinary research that takes advantage of UG's distinct advantages and top-tier academic institutions in many fields. Applicants with innovative research interests that have the potential to revolutionize computer science and other fields will be given priority in all areas.

We urge applicants to submit their applications as soon as they can. Complete consideration will be given to applications received by December 10, 2023; however, candidates may be invited for interviews beginning in November. The posts won't be filled until they are. On a rolling basis, applications received after December 10, 2023, will be reviewed.

Applicants should submit

  1. A cover letter
  2. A curriculum vitae
  3. Three to five letters of reference
  4. A statement of research interests
  5. A statement of teaching philosophy
  6. A personal statement that addresses how the candidate contributes to diverse excellence
  7. Two representative publications.

Computer Science Assistant Professor of Instruction/Lecturer

For non-tenure-track teaching positions as lecturers and faculty in all fields of computer science, the University of Ghana Computer Science is looking for exceptional applicants. UG CS is a vibrant, expanding academic community that is home to undergraduate, graduate, and postgraduate students. The chosen candidate will be an exceptional mentor and teacher who combines a strong background in computer science with a love of sharing that knowledge with a wide range of learners. In order to build learning experiences that inspire students to enroll, study, learn, and discover new applications of computer science that will impact their jobs and the wider world, they will go far beyond simply offering entry-level service courses. The initial appointment terms for this multi-year, renewable job at the Assistant Professor rank are three to five years. Our goal is to help new faculty to build lasting and meaningful careers within a supportive community that values teaching and learning.

Candidates must hold a Ph.D. in computer science or a closely related discipline in order to be considered for the Assistant Professor of Instruction post. A master's degree in computer science and a proven track record in instructing computer science are prerequisites for the lecturer post. Usually, faculty members instruct two courses every term with substantial assistance from graduate and undergraduate teaching assistants. They also work on departmental curriculum development and student advice.

Applicants must submit

  1. A cover letter
  2. A curriculum vitae
  3. Three to five letters of reference which can speak to the applicant's teaching abilities
  4. A statement of teaching philosophy
  5. A diversity statement that addresses how the candidate contributes to inclusive, accessible, and equitable educational environments.
  6. Recent teaching evaluations (if available)
  7. A teaching demonstration video (if available)

We encourage candidates to send applications as soon as possible. We will begin to reveiw applications on October 25, 2023. However the position will remain open until filled.

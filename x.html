CS+X INNITIATIVE

CS+X at University of Ghana

What is CS+X?

The possibilities of multidisciplinary study are exploded by University of Ghana's Computer Science + X (CS+X) project.
Establishing links throughout the University and fostering transformative interactions between computer science and other subjects are our goals.


Students and Lecturers are pushing the limits of computer science through CS+X, which has the potential to inspire whole new academic disciplines. Through courses relevant to non-majors, undergraduate students from many departments within the institution make the connection between computing and their academic interests. The goal of the PhD program in computer science at University of Ghana is to inspire students to work with advisers in computer science and other fields, as well as to explore wide research interests. Faculty in computer science form multidisciplinary research groups that span departments and schools and work with outside partners and businesses.

CS+X Workshops and Events

Ongoing activities

Artificial Intelligence at UG (AI@UG): A community of researchers, educators, and students across Northwestern are advancing artificial intelligence in theory and practice.

CS+X Colloquium Series: The colloquium series seeks to define the challenges and opportunities for computer science and other disciplines as nature of computational thinking continue to evolve.

CS+Buffett Series: The Buffett Institute for Global Affairs and the CS Department explore the intersection of computer science and global studies and examine the ways in which computing and data science can be applied to our greatest global challenges.

CS+Economics: Experts at the intersection of computer science and economics present their perspective and research during thematic CS+Econ workshop events.

CS+Law Monthly Workshop: The special interest group creates a forum for exchanging ideas in a collegial environment that promotes building community, collaboration, and research that helps to further develop CS+Law as a field.

Institute for Data, Econometrics, Algorithms, and Learning (IDEAL): A team of interdisciplinary investigators and study the theoretical foundations related to high dimensional data analysis, data science in strategic environments, and machine learning and optimization.

Machine Learning Impact Initiative: The Machine Learning Impact Initiative aims to bring together a network of researchers and professionals to develop a research roadmap of the potential impacts of machine learning on human health and safety and to identify approaches to evaluate risk in ML systems from data to deployment.
